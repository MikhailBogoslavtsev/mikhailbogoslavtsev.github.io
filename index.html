About

How I work

I've spent thirteen-plus years in B2B SaaS, the last several in construction tech, turning operational problems that look impossibly messy from the outside into software people rely on every day.

I didn't start in product. I spent a decade in technical sales across Eastern Europe — sitting with customers on factory floors long before I ever wrote a product spec. That shaped how I lead more than any framework did: I trust what I see in the field over what sounds good in a meeting room, and I'd rather build the second version of something real than the first version of something theoretical.

A few things I believe:

  • Outcomes over output. Shipping a lot isn't the goal. Moving something that matters to the business and the customer is. I'd rather ship three things that change the trajectory than thirty that fill a changelog.
  • Love the problem, not the solution. The fastest way to waste a quarter is to fall for your own idea. I keep teams anchored to the problem we're solving and the people we're solving it for, and stay honest when the first answer turns out to be wrong.
  • Give teams the “why” and room to solve it. The best work I've seen came from teams who understood the goal deeply and were trusted to figure out the how. My job as a leader is to make the direction unmistakable and then get out of the way.
  • Bridge the technical and the commercial. I've spent time on both sides — engineering rooms and customer negotiations — and a lot of my value is translating between them so the product, the business, and the people building it are actually pulling the same direction.

Whether I'm leading product full-time, stepping in as a fractional Head of Product, or mentoring someone through a hard call, the through-line is the same: get close to the real problem, make the direction clear, and help good people do their best work.
